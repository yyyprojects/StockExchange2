The Two Titans of the USA

In the U.S., trading is dominated by two household names: the NYSE and the Nasdaq.

Who Has More Listings?

Nasdaq lists more companies, especially from the tech sector.

Who Has a Larger Market Value?

The total value of NYSE-listed companies is slightly larger.

New York Stock Exchange (NYSE)

Founded: 1792

Trading Type: Auction Market (Hybrid of floor traders and electronic)

Known For: Large, established "blue-chip" companies and industrials.

Nasdaq

Founded: 1971

Trading Type: Dealer's Market (Fully electronic)

Known For: Technology, biotech, and innovative growth companies.

The Market's Referee: The SEC

The Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) is the U.S. government agency that acts as the ultimate watchdog to ensure fairness and protect investors.

Enforces the Rules

It fights against fraud, insider trading, and market manipulation to keep the playing field level.

Demands Transparency

It requires public companies to disclose their financial information regularly and truthfully.

Protects You

Its primary mission is to protect everyday investors and maintain confidence in the market.
